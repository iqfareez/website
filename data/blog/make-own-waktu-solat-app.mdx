## What you'll need

- Flutter SDK
- IDE

First, you'll need a Flutter ready on your machine. This part will not be covered in this article. You can read the [official documentation](https://docs.flutter.dev/get-started/install) or go to YouTube to help you kick off. Next, you'll need an IDE, or in other words, a place to write and debug your code. I'll be using [Visual Studio Code](https://code.visualstudio.com) for this tutorial (not to be confused with [Microsoft Visual Studio](https://visualstudio.microsoft.com/vs/)).
